Research Question and What This Campaign Must Answer

Original Question

Can induction-head targeting/steering improve arithmetic performance in LLMs?

Updated Mainline Question

Are arithmetic failures better explained by operator-specific bottlenecks plus control/gating limits than by induction-head amplification alone?

Required evidence from this run

Non-trivial localization, operator-specific intervention effects, and substantive CoT evidence under preregistered gates.

What We Did (Plain English)

Step 1: Test the original idea

We first tested whether pushing induction-head behavior could directly improve arithmetic.

Step 2: Validate the tools

We made sure hooks, detectors, and steering interventions actually changed the model in controlled settings.

Step 3: Reframe the bottleneck

Because arithmetic gains were mostly null/mixed, we pivoted to operator-specific bottlenecks (add/sub/mul).

Step 4: Run a stricter campaign

We are now running a hardened Phase 2 campaign that requires stronger causal and statistical evidence.

Simple summary: Phase 1 validated the measurement/control machinery; Phase 2 tests where arithmetic failure actually lives.

Phase 2 Program Design + Live Campaign Configuration

Phase 2 is not just "run more experiments." It is a targeted attempt to identify which internal components are responsible for arithmetic subskills and whether those components are operator-specific.

What Phase 2 Is Trying to Accomplish

Goal A: Find real arithmetic components

  • Find attention/MLP components that have measurable causal effect on arithmetic outputs.
  • Reject "effects" that are too small, unstable, or likely noise.

Goal B: Show operator specificity

  • Components picked for addition should matter more for addition than for subtraction/multiplication.
  • This is the key test that the bottleneck is operator-specific, not generic disruption.

Goal C: Explain CoT gains mechanistically

  • Test whether CoT changes circuit recruitment in a meaningful way.
  • Rule out trivial explanation: "CoT only changes formatting/parse rate."

If Phase 2 passes

We get evidence-backed operator bottlenecks and a clear next step for multi-model replication.

If Phase 2 fails

That is still valuable: it narrows the hypothesis and tells us to move to broader/path-level interventions.

Results We Hope to See From the Current Campaign

Primary success pattern (academically meaningful)

  • Localization shows non-trivial effects for both attention and MLP on at least one operator family.
  • Top-set interventions beat matched-random on preregistered rows with signed directionality.
  • Specificity holds against non-target operators (not just target-only gains).
  • CoT evidence passes with effect not explained by parse-rate shifts.
  • Readiness only becomes true when all required gates pass without scope blocks.

If results are mixed or null

  • Treat as informative null: bottlenecks may be broader than current component families.
  • Prioritize targeted patching/path-level interventions and expanded robustness checks.
  • Keep interpretation conservative: no positive operator-mechanism claim without gate-complete evidence.

Decision Gates and Thresholds (Current Campaign)

This table is the concrete contract for interpretation. If any required row fails, the campaign is not ready for multi-model conclusions.

Gate Must show Fail condition
dataset_bucket_gate Valid bucket construction + split policy applied Strict split leakage or insufficient bucket quality
localization_validity_gate Non-negligible effects + robustness coverage for enabled component families (attention + MLP) Fails if effects do not clear configured floors or robustness is missing
operator_specificity_gate Preregistered row-level target > non-target evidence with CI support Missing non-target evidence, weak CI support, or multiplicity failure
intervention_sanity_gate No unexplained extreme anomalies Flagged impossible jumps/regressions
cot_gating_evidence_gate Substantive CoT effect not reducible to parse-rate shift CoT disabled or effect explained by formatting/parse artifacts

How to read this quickly

  • First check gate pass/fail status, not raw metric highs.
  • Then inspect failure reason fields and scope blocks.
  • Only after that interpret accuracy deltas and qualitative patterns.

Why thresholds matter

  • They prevent post-hoc threshold shopping.
  • They enforce consistency across Llama/Gemma/GPT-2 runs.
  • They make null results interpretable rather than ambiguous.

Key thresholds from config: localization_nonzero_min=0.01, localization_prob_delta_abs_min_floor=1e-5, specificity_ci_low_min=0.01, specificity_mean_gap_min=0.01, multiplicity q_max=0.10, CoT effect_abs_min=0.01, parse_rate_delta_abs_max=0.05.

Why These Gates Exist (Plain Language)

The gates are not just engineering checks. They are the minimum requirements for a defensible mechanistic claim.

1) Dataset gate

What it checks: we built the right prompt buckets and properly separated selection vs evaluation data.

Why needed: without this, later results can be data leakage, not mechanism.

2) Localization gate

What it checks: measured effects are non-trivial and stable across robustness modes.

Why needed: tiny/noisy effects cannot justify component-level claims.

3) Specificity gate

What it checks: preregistered top-set effects are stronger on target operator than non-target operators.

Why needed: this is the core test of the Phase 2 hypothesis (operator-specific bottlenecks).

4) Intervention sanity gate

What it checks: no implausible jumps/regressions suggesting parser or hook artifacts.

Why needed: prevents accepting broken-but-impressive looking numbers.

5) CoT gate

What it checks: CoT effects are substantive and not explained only by parse formatting.

Why needed: CoT gating/composition is a central Phase 2 claim and must be tested directly.

Readiness requires all required gates to pass together. A single strong number is not enough.
